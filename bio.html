What qualifies me as a game designer?

Designing games is the art of constructing experiences and I had the fortune of traveling in my life. The experience of meeting new people, making friends, going on adventures, and finding new things to enjoy, these provides a strong background to draw from to enable the creation of engaging games. To be a great designer you need good communication skills, to work with a team of individuals with amazing ideas. You must provide direction for the team while listening, considering their inputs and the respective costs and benefits of the implementation. I spent four years as a Residential Assistant, which involved a lot of mediating/counselling of those within my building. To effectively help those who sought me out, I learned how to really listen. I needed to ensure that all sides were understood and understood how I weighted the options available before providing my advice. Having experience in these matters has allowed me to develop communication skills that excel in cooperative environments.

How did I get here?

Since high school, I have had a desire to create experiences. The medium has not always been games; I was into acting and directing in high school. In university, I took a game design class and became entranced with the impact games have, and how I could be a creator. Since then I have been improving all aspects that would be applicable to designing games. I am making board games, video games, music, writing and art and I will continue to improve my skill set.
